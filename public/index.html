About Fallen Angels

Fallen Angels Inc. is a nonprofit organization committed to elevating Armenian culture on the global stage through the power of art. Our mission is to deepen awareness, understanding, and appreciation of Armenia’s rich cultural heritage—both across the worldwide Armenian diaspora and within the broader international arts community.

Through exhibitions, artistic collaborations, and cultural initiatives, we champion creative expression as a bridge between communities and a catalyst for shared democratic values. We support artists, institutions, and educational programs that foster dialogue and cross-cultural exchange, ensuring that Armenian voices remain vibrant, visible, and influential within today’s cultural landscape.

Current Projects

Fallen Angels is honored to lead and support major international projects, including the Armenian Pavilion at the Venice Biennale 2026, advancing Armenia’s artistic innovation at one of the most significant and respected cultural platforms in the world.

Armenian Pavilion at Venice Biennale 2026

Oct 26, 2025

Zadik Zadikian to Represent Armenia

Fallen Angels Inc. is honored to announce that the Republic of Armenia will be represented at the 2026 Venice Biennale by artist Zadik Zadikian, whose visionary practice spans decades of sculptural innovation and a lifelong dialogue with material, light, and the metaphysics of artistic making. In alignment with the Biennale’s curatorial theme In Minor Keys—an invitation to create spaces of reflection, resonance, and poetic attunement—Zadikian will transform the Armenian Pavilion into a living, breathing studio: a site of continual making, presence, and shared experience.

Cafesjian Center for the Arts - Yerevan - Fall 2024 Show

May 6, 2024

Fallen Angels Monument - Coming in Fall 2024

Layering Reason - is a monument by artist Zadik Zadikian as a dedication from the Armenian community to the fallen soldiers of the 2020 war in Nagorno-Karabakh that took the lives of thousands of Armenian soldiers.

For Armenians around the globe, this is a tragic topic. The goal of this monument is to immortalize the fallen soldiers and give the viewer a visceral experience of the scale of loss inflicted by the conflict. Along with this, the piece aims to be a work of hope.
